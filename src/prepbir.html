THE PERFECT BIRYANI

LAYERING THE BIRYANI

STEPS BEFORE STARTING THE STOVE

  • IN A THICK-BOTTOMED VESSEL OR BIRYANI HANDI, GREASE THE BOTTOM WITH GHEE.
  • START WITH A LAYER OF THE COOKED MARINATED CHICKEN.
  • NEXT, SPREAD A LAYER OF PARTIALLY COOKED BASMATI RICE (ABOUT 60–70% COOKED).
  • SPRINKLE SOME FRIED ONIONS (BIRISTA) OVER THE RICE.
  • ADD CHOPPED MINT AND CORIANDER LEAVES.
  • DRIZZLE A MIX OF SAFFRON MILK OR YELLOW FOOD COLOR SOAKED IN WARM MILK.
  • ADD A FEW TEASPOONS OF WARM GHEE EVENLY OVER THE TOP.
  • REPEAT THE LAYERING IF NEEDED — RICE → BIRISTA → HERBS → GHEE.
  • ENSURE THE TOPMOST LAYER IS RICE WITH GHEE AND SAFFRON.
  • COVER THE VESSEL WITH A TIGHT LID OR SEAL WITH DOUGH TO TRAP STEAM.
  • COOK THE BIRYANI FOR 5 MINUTES ON HIGH FLAME AND THEN TURN TO SIMMER AND START THE TIMER.
PRO TIP: SPRINKLE A LITTLE WATER AROUND THE EDGES BEFORE SEALING THE POT TO CREATE MORE STEAM. ALWAYS LET THE BIRYANI SIT UNDISTURBED FOR 5–10 MINUTES AFTER DUM FOR BEST RESULTS.
